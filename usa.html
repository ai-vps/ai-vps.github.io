Cost Optimization Strategies

Reserved Commitments

AWS Savings Plans and Azure Reserved VMs can reduce compute costs by 30-69%. Commit to 1-year terms for a good balance of savings and flexibility.

Spot / Preemptible Instances

For fault-tolerant workloads, spot instances offer 60-90% discounts. Use them for batch processing, model training checkpoints, and dev/test environments.

Multi-Model Strategy

Route simple queries to cheap models (GPT-4o-mini, Gemini Flash) and complex ones to frontier models. This can reduce AI API costs by 60-80%.

Get Started

Your Path to
AI Infrastructure

Choosing the right cloud and AI providers is one of the most consequential technical decisions your team will make. Here is a practical framework for making that decision with confidence.

Decision Framework

  1. Audit Your Workloads

    Map your current and planned workloads: compute, storage, AI inference, training, data analytics. Different providers excel at different workload profiles. If 80% of your spend is GPU inference, a specialized GPU provider may beat a hyperscaler.

  2. Define Your Constraints

    Data residency requirements, compliance certifications (SOC2, HIPAA, GDPR, PIPL), latency to end users, existing vendor relationships, and team skills all narrow the field. China deployments require domestic providers (citation:4).

  3. Evaluate AI Integration Needs

    Do you need a managed model API (OpenAI, Anthropic), a platform for fine-tuning (Vertex AI, SageMaker), or self-hosted open-weight models (Llama, Qwen)? This choice fundamentally shapes your provider strategy.

  4. Run Cost Modelling

    Model your expected usage against each provider's pricing. Include compute, storage, bandwidth, AI API calls, and support costs. Ask for enterprise discounts — list prices are starting points, not final prices. Consider 3-year reserved pricing for stable workloads (citation:1).

  5. Start Small, Scale Deliberately

    Begin with a single workload on your shortlisted provider. Measure real-world performance, cost, and developer experience for 30-60 days before committing further. Keep exit costs in mind — architect for portability where practical.
